About

I am currently a Metallurgical and Materials Engineering student at the University of Texas at El Paso, expecting to graduate in May 2027.

Most recently, I completed an 8-month co-op as a quality metallurgist at Nucor, where I performed failure analysis and root cause investigations, used advanced microscopy techniques to understand defect formation, and worked cross-functionally to implement quality improvements that prevented customer claims.

In my past lives, I have conducted research in high school for magnetically actuated millirobot development at SMU (2019), worked as a legislative intern at the National Mining Association (2024) analyzing how environmental regulations affect critical minerals supply chains, as well as spent over a year as a research assistant developing self-healing polymer blends at UTEP's Polymer Extrusion Lab (2023-2024) for NSF-funded additive manufacturing research.

Summer Research Student
SMU BAST Lab â€“ Dallas, TX | July 2019 â€“ Aug 2019
I spent the summer exploring the potential of magnetically actuated millirobots for minimally invasive medical procedures. I designed these robots using 3D-printed PLA structures with embedded permanent magnets, carefully considering how magnetic field interactions could enable controlled locomotion at the millimeter scale. Through systematic testing of three different locomotion modes (pivot walking, tumbling, and rolling) I determined that pivot walking achieved 40% faster speeds while providing more controlled navigation through complex environments. Fabricating these prototypes using fused deposition modeling required maintaining 0.1mm dimensional tolerances to ensure precise magnet placement and consistent magnetic response for reliable locomotion control. I validated navigation capabilities by guiding the robots through maze environments using electromagnetic coil control systems, demonstrating the feasibility of remotely controlled microrobots for applications like targeted drug delivery. Throughout the summer, I collaborated with graduate students to integrate my experimental findings into the lab's broader medical robotics research program, contributing to their development of next-generation surgical tools.

Making

Because it is immensely satisfying to work with materials that have predictable mechanical properties...

I make clothes. I am currently obsessed with tailoring and fitting, having discovered that understanding how woven fabrics behave along the bias versus the straight grain is surprisingly relevant to getting sleeves to set properly and darts to lie flat.

I have made a variety of things, like blazers that mostly fit around the shoulders, wrap dresses with questionable closure engineering, and experimental structured pieces that looked better as technical flats than as actual garments, as well as numerous toiles and fitting muslins that I have no pictures of because before 2025, I hadn't yet accepted that documentation might actually help me remember why certain adjustments worked.
